Martial arts encompass a wide range of combat practices designed for self-defense, physical fitness, discipline, and competitive sport, each with its own techniques, philosophies, and cultural origins. What Are Martial Arts? Martial arts are systems of combat techniques originally developed for military and self-defense purposes but now practiced worldwide for fitness, sport, and mental discipline. They emphasize skills such as striking, grappling, weaponry, and strategy. Common Types of Martial Arts Karate: Originating from Japan, focusing on striking techniques like punches, kicks, and blocks. Taekwondo: A Korean martial art known for its dynamic kicking techniques. Judo: A Japanese form emphasizing throws and ground grappling. Kung Fu (Wushu): Traditional Chinese martial arts with various styles that combine fluid movements and self-defense. Muay Thai: Thai boxing focusing on strikes with fists, elbows, knees, and shins. Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu: A grappling-based martial art with emphasis on ground fighting and submissions. Silambam and Kalaripayattu: Traditional Indian martial arts with origins in Maharashtra, Kerala, and other regions, focusing on weapon training and fluid combat forms.
