Mahaveer Jayanti

Mahaveer Jayanti 2025 is celebrated on April 10, marking the 2623rd birth anniversary of Lord Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism, honoring his teachings of non-violence, truth, and spiritual discipline. Date and Background Mahaveer Jayanti in 2025 falls on Thursday, April 10, according to the Jain calendar on the Trayodashi Tithi (thirteenth day) of the bright half of the month of Chaitra. Lord Mahavira was born in 599 BCE in Kundalagrama (near present-day Vaishali, Bihar) to King Siddhartha and Queen Trishala. He was originally named Vardhamana, meaning "one who grows," symbolizing prosperity and spiritual growth. At age 30, he renounced his royal life to attain Kevala Jnana (omniscience) through 12 years of rigorous meditation and asceticism, eventually founding Jainism as it is known today Vajiram & Ravi +2 . Significance Mahaveer Jayanti is one of the most sacred and important festivals for Jains worldwide as it commemorates the birth of their last Tirthankara, Lord Mahavira. His teachings emphasize: Ahimsa (Non-Violence): Respecting all life forms and avoiding harm in thought, word, or deed. Satya (Truthfulness): Always speaking and living truthfully. Asteya (Non-Stealing): Not taking anything not willingly given. Brahmacharya (Celibacy or self-discipline): Control over desires and senses. Aparigraha (Non-Possessiveness): Detachment from material possessions and greed. These core values guide followers on the path to liberation (Moksha), which Lord Mahavira achieved at the age of 72 Vajiram & Ravi +3 . How Mahaveer Jayanti is Celebrated Temple Visits and Prayers: Devotees visit Jain temples to offer prayers, perform Abhisheka—the ceremonial bathing of Mahavira's idol with milk, water, and honey—and participate in pujas and religious discourses. Rath Yatra (Chariot Processions): Grand processions featuring the idol of Lord Mahavira carried on elaborately decorated chariots through the streets, accompanied by devotional songs, chanting, and community participation. Fasting and Meditation: Many observe fasting and dedicate the day to meditation and spiritual reflection, fostering self-discipline and inner purity. Charity and Acts of Kindness: Following Lord Mahavira’s message of compassion, devotees engage in charitable work including feeding the poor, donating clothes, and supporting the underprivileged. Satvik Diet: Consumption of freshly prepared vegetarian food, free from onions and garlic, to uphold purity and non-violence in diet. The festival is observed not only across India—including major centers like Pawapuri, Girnar, Palitana, and Shri Mahavirji temple—but also by Jain diaspora worldwide, spreading Lord Mahavira's message of peace and ethical living Vajiram & Ravi +3 . Spiritual Essence and Teachings Mahaveer Jayanti reaffirms Lord Mahavira's life teachings that embrace tolerance, truth, and non-attachment. The festival especially encourages practicing forgiveness, self-purification, and renunciation to move closer toward spiritual liberation. Additionally, during the sacred month when Paryushan takes place (August–September), the principles of Mahavira continue to inspire reflection and fasting among Jains, extending the spirit of his birth celebration India TV News . In Summary Mahaveer Jayanti is a vibrant celebration of the birth and legacy of Lord Mahavira, inspiring millions through his timeless ideals of peace, non-violence, and truth. Observed with devotion, rituals, and community service, it serves as a reminder of spiritual awakening and ethical living, making it one of the most cherished festivals in Jain culture and Indian heritage.
